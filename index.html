Guidelines & FAQ

Please read before submitting.

We're documenting illegal dumping across the Mojave Desert and Antelope Valley in Kern, Los Angeles, Riverside, and San Bernardino Counties. We're looking for:
  1. Illegal dump sites — piles of trash, tires, construction debris, hazardous materials on desert land.
  2. Trucks or vehicles that appear to be actively dumping material.
  3. Evidence of large-scale or organized dumping operations.

If you photograph a truck, please ensure license plates and company logos are clearly visible.
Every photo must be taken from a public road or public land. Do not trespass on private property. Do not follow vehicles or put yourself in danger. Maintain a safe and legal distance at all times.
This tool accepts submissions from the desert communities of Kern, Los Angeles, Riverside, and San Bernardino Counties — including the Antelope Valley, the Mojave Desert, and surrounding high-desert areas. Submissions from outside this region will not be accepted.
The app reads GPS coordinates embedded in your photo's metadata. If those aren't present (or for videos), it asks your device for your current location. Submissions must include verifiable location data.

To ensure GPS is in your photos: iPhone → Settings → Privacy → Location Services → Camera → "While Using." Android → Camera app settings → enable Location tags.
We reserve the right to use submitted photos and videos in our reporting. Submissions may also be shared with regulatory agencies or law enforcement. Your contact info will never be published.
Submitting without contact info is effectively anonymous. We strip personal metadata before any public use. If you have safety concerns, note them in the description or reach out via Signal at jacobmargolis.01 before submitting. Your safety comes first.
For sensitive tips, reach us directly on Signal at jacobmargolis.01 — Signal messages are end-to-end encrypted. You can also email us at editor@yourpublication.com.
