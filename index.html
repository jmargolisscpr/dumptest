Guidelines & FAQ

Please read before submitting.

We're documenting illegal dumping across the Mojave Desert and Antelope Valley in Kern, Los Angeles, Riverside, and San Bernardino Counties. We're looking for:
  1. Illegal dump sites — piles of trash, tires, construction debris, hazardous materials on desert land.
  2. Trucks or vehicles that appear to be actively dumping material.
  3. Evidence of large-scale or organized dumping operations.

If you photograph a truck, please ensure license plates and company logos are clearly visible.
Every photo must be taken from a public road or public land. Do not trespass on private property. Do not follow vehicles or put yourself in danger. Maintain a safe and legal distance at all times.
For now we're explicitly documenting illegal dumping across the Los Angeles, San Bernardino, Kern and Riverside county desert areas — including the Antelope Valley, the Mojave Desert, and surrounding high-desert areas. Submissions from outside this region will not be accepted.
When you upload a file we'll securely log the location data associated with each photo and video. We won't accept submissions without that information, as it's critical to verifying the veracity of the evidence.

To ensure GPS is in your photos: iPhone → Settings → Privacy → Location Services → Camera → "While Using."

Android → Camera app settings → enable Location tags.
We reserve the right to use submitted photos and videos in our reporting, however we won't include your name without your permission. We're less likely to include it without supplemental information. Your contact info will never be published.
We strip personal metadata before any public use. If you have safety concerns, note them in the description.
You can reach the reporter on Signal at jacobmargolis.01. You can also email him at jmargolis@scpr.org with the subject line "Dumping Submission."
